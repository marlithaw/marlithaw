About Me

I lead with a systems-focused approach—creating learning ecosystems that cultivate growth, strengthen capacity, and transform lived experiences into sustainable leadership. My journey began as a Teach For America corps member and evolved through campus leadership, law, and literacy advocacy. Stepping into a caregiving role this year further deepened my belief that supportive systems and community are the real foundations of transformative leadership and equity.

Philosophy

  • Systemic change beats “superstar” solutions every time.
  • Growing teachers and leaders is sustainable only with personalized support.
  • Equity, healing, and data-driven progress can (and must) co-exist in education.

Credentials

  • Juris Doctor — University of Arkansas School of Law
  • Bachelor’s — Purdue University
  • Texas Bar, Licensed since 2016
  • Reading Academies Leadership Pathway Certified, 2024

3-year 5th Grade Outcomes Overview

Over three years (2022–2024), my 5th graders consistently outperformed district and state benchmarks in STAAR Reading and Writing—even when staff turnover and pandemic disruptions posed major challenges. My campus approach combined research-based literacy frameworks, targeted coaching, and focused professional learning to create powerful, real-world results.

  • Writing Process Skills: +16% above state
  • Editing & Punctuation: +11% above state
  • Reading Comprehension: +4% above state
