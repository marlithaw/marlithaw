Structured Literacy

Expert implementation of science-based reading instruction

Science of Reading Approach

Structured literacy is an approach to reading instruction that is systematic, explicit, and cumulative. It focuses on teaching the fundamental components of reading in a way that is backed by scientific research on how the brain learns to read.

Phonics-based early literacy

My approach emphasizes systematic phonics instruction that teaches students the relationship between letters and sounds, enabling them to decode unfamiliar words independently. This foundation is crucial for reading fluency and comprehension in later grades.

  • Systematic and explicit phonics instruction
  • Sequential introduction of sound-spelling relationships
  • Regular practice with decodable texts

Reading comprehension frameworks

Beyond decoding, I implement structured approaches to develop deep reading comprehension, teaching students to actively engage with texts through questioning, summarizing, and analyzing.

  • Text-dependent questioning techniques
  • Close reading protocols
  • Comprehension strategy instruction

Writing across content areas

Writing reinforces reading skills and deepens content knowledge. My framework integrates writing instruction across all subject areas, providing students with tools to organize their thoughts and express ideas clearly.

  • Explicit instruction in writing structures
  • Evidence-based writing in response to reading
  • Grammar and mechanics taught in context

Impact & Results

Implementation of these structured literacy approaches resulted in 80% of 5th grade students meeting or exceeding yearly reading growth targets for three consecutive years, even during challenging instructional conditions.
