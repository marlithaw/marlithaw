Sustainable Instructional Leadership

Driving Excellence Without a Dedicated Teacher

A case study of IDEA Brackenridge Academy's 5th Grade Reading/Writing Achievement

Marlitha Williams | Educational Leader & Instructional Coach

The Challenge

No Dedicated 5th Grade Reading Teacher

During the 2023-2024 academic year, IDEA Brackenridge Academy faced a significant staffing challenge with no dedicated reading teacher for 5th grade students.

High-Stakes Expectations

Despite this challenge, the campus was still expected to maintain high academic performance on the state assessments that incorporated both reading and writing skills.

How Was This Achieved?

The Instructional Framework

"The instructional framework established was so robust and systematic that it enabled consistent, high-quality instruction even when delivered by substitute teachers under my guidance."

The success was driven by a comprehensive instructional framework that created consistency and coherence across all literacy instruction.

Vertically Aligned Instruction

  • Consistent terminology across grade levels
  • Clear skill progression documented for teachers
  • Common visual supports and instructional routines
  • Research-based literacy approaches

Science of Reading & Writing

  • Integration of reading and writing instruction
  • Explicit teaching of language structures
  • Focus on both foundational and higher-order skills
  • Systematic application of literacy principles

Comprehensive Curriculum

Detailed lesson materials that could be effectively delivered by any teacher with minimal preparation.

Strategic Intervention

Data-driven small group interventions that targeted specific skills based on ongoing assessment.

Consistent Monitoring

Regular progress monitoring and instructional adjustments based on student performance data.

Implementation Strategies

1

Substitute Teacher Support System

Created detailed lesson plans with explicit guidance, exemplars, and anticipated student challenges to support substitute teachers.

2

Strategic Intervention Blocks

Implemented targeted small-group instruction during intervention blocks to address skill gaps and accelerate growth, particularly in writing.

3

Gradual Release Instructional Model

Applied a consistent "I Do, We Do, You Do" approach that made instruction predictable and effective, regardless of the teacher.

4

Vertically Aligned Skill Instruction

Created consistent terminology and instructional routines that built upon skills learned in previous grades, particularly focused on the integration of reading and writing.

Key Insights & Leadership Implications

The Power of Systems Over Personalities

The most significant insight from this case study is that well-designed instructional systems can maintain excellence even without a dedicated content teacher. This demonstrates that sustainable educational leadership is about building systems that transcend individual personalities.

When instructional leaders focus on creating robust frameworks, detailed resources, and clear implementation guidelines, they create lasting impact that extends beyond their direct presence.

Evidence of System Strength

  • Consistent performance across two years
  • Excellence in complex skills that require strong instruction
  • Substitute teachers able to implement effectively
  • Strongest performance in writing, which is typically most vulnerable to teacher quality

Leadership Takeaways

  • Invest in comprehensive curriculum and support materials
  • Create clear instructional routines that any teacher can follow
  • Develop monitoring systems that identify and address gaps quickly
  • Build vertical alignment that creates coherence across grade levels
