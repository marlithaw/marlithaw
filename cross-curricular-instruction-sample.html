Comprehensive Cross-Curricular Approach: Precision with Prepositions

A holistic approach to integrated instruction across content areas

Instructional Approach Overview

This comprehensive cross-curricular approach provides a focused strategy for reinforcing prepositional phrases across grades 3-5 as one element within a holistic literacy curriculum. Rather than treating prepositional phrases as isolated grammar skills, this approach connects them directly to author's purposeâ€”recognizing that writers strategically use these phrases to control precision, perspective, and emotional impact in their communication.

By integrating instruction across multiple content areas (ELA, Math, Science, Reading, and SEL), students experience how prepositional phrases serve as essential building blocks for achieving specific communicative goals while simultaneously developing skills across domains.

This instructional design is built on three interconnected principles:

Grammar as Purposeful Communication Tool

Prepositional phrases serve as essential building blocks for achieving author's purpose, not merely as grammatical elements to be mastered. Students analyze how writers use prepositional choices to control precision, perspective, and emotional impact.

Meaningful Cross-Curricular Integration

Learning experiences are designed to serve multiple curricular goals simultaneously, creating deeper connections across subject areas while making grammar instruction relevant and contextual.

Developmental Skill Progression

Instruction follows a research-based sequence that moves from identification to creation to analysis, with complexity increasing as students progress from concrete spatial relationships to nuanced author's craft evaluation.

Additionally, this approach is enhanced by these supporting elements:

Multiple Access Points

Following UDL principles, lessons provide various ways for students to engage with content, regardless of learning style or background.

Teacher Support

Embedded coaching notes guide teachers in adapting instruction responsively, making high-quality teaching accessible to educators at all experience levels.

Whole-Child Approach

Academic content and social-emotional learning are seamlessly integrated, recognizing that effective learning addresses both domains simultaneously.

Assessment-Informed Instruction

Formative assessment tools track student progress with both skill mastery and cross-curricular application to drive responsive teaching decisions.

Implementation Guidance

Successful implementation of this cross-curricular approach requires thoughtful planning and intentional integration. The following steps guide educators in implementing this approach effectively:

1

Begin with Author's Purpose

Before addressing grammar rules, establish clear understanding of how prepositional phrases serve as tools for achieving specific communication goals. Connect to grade-level standards across content areas.

Analyze mentor texts for prepositional precision and purpose
Discuss how author choices influence reader understanding and experience
2

Identify Meaningful Cross-Curricular Connections

Review your curriculum maps to find organic connections between prepositional phrases and concepts in math, science, reading, and SEL.

Look for content that naturally uses spatial, temporal, or manner relationships
Consider current units of study in other content areas for natural integration
3

Prepare Visual Vocabulary Supports

Create clear visual representations of key prepositional concepts, ensuring they support all learners, especially ELLs.

Use consistent imagery across lessons to build understanding
Connect abstract prepositions to concrete, relatable examples
4

Maintain Consistent Structure

Follow the 60-minute structure consistently to build student familiarity with the routine.

Opening (5 min)

SEL connection and purpose setting

Visual Vocabulary (5 min)

Clear introduction of key terms

Core ELA Activity (30 min)

Direct instruction and guided practice

Integrated Practice (10 min)

Cross-curricular application

Reflection (5 min)

Metacognitive processing

Assessment (5 min)

Quick check for understanding

5

Use Formative Assessment Data Daily

The mastery tracker provided in each lesson should be used to guide instructional decisions for subsequent lessons.

Use data to form flexible groups for the next day's instruction
Note patterns across multiple skills to identify comprehensive learning needs

Research Foundation

The Comprehensive Cross-Curricular Approach is grounded in established research across multiple educational domains, synthesizing evidence-based practices into a holistic instructional design.

Science of Reading & Writing Connection

This approach draws from Dr. Young-Suk Kim's research on the interdependence of reading and writing development, particularly the finding that syntactic knowledge (like understanding prepositional phrases) supports both reading comprehension and written expression.

By explicitly teaching prepositional phrases as tools for achieving author's purpose within authentic contexts, students develop the metalinguistic awareness that strengthens both decoding and encoding processes.

Universal Design for Learning

The instructional design implements UDL principles by providing:

  • Multiple means of representation: Visual vocabulary, physical demonstrations, and verbal explanations
  • Multiple means of action and expression: Various response options from simple identification to complex analysis
  • Multiple means of engagement: Integration with interesting content and personal connections through SEL

Integrated Curriculum Research

Research by Hinde (2005) and Vars & Beane (2000) demonstrates that integrated curriculum approaches lead to:

  • Deeper conceptual understanding across subjects
  • Improved transfer of learning to new contexts
  • Greater student engagement and motivation
  • More efficient use of instructional time

Author's Purpose and Grammar Instruction

Research by Weaver (1996) and Graham & Perin (2007) shows that grammar instruction is most effective when:

  • Connected to authentic writing purposes rather than taught in isolation
  • Framed as tools for achieving specific effects rather than rules to follow
  • Taught through examples in mentor texts that demonstrate effective use
  • Practiced in meaningful contexts that connect to student experiences

Grade-Level Lesson Plans

The following comprehensive 60-minute lesson plans demonstrate the Comprehensive Cross-Curricular Approach in action across grades 3-5, showing how prepositional phrases serve as tools for achieving author's purpose while integrating across content areas.

Grade 3: Preposition Discovery

A foundational lesson focusing on identifying "where" prepositional phrases, integrated with spatial math concepts, habitat science, and location-based emotional expression.

Grade 4: Prepositional Precision

An application-focused lesson on creating prepositional phrases for different functions (where, when, how), connected to fractions, forces, and personal growth narratives.

Grade 5: Prepositional Purpose

An analytical lesson examining how authors use prepositional phrases for specific effects, integrated with proportion concepts, scientific procedures, and precise emotional language.
