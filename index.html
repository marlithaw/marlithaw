Anchor Text Options for Integrated Literacy Intervention

The following 40 texts are organized by complexity level and feature various affixes aligned with Indiana's 3rd grade standards. Each text includes identified target affixes, Lexile level, and main idea/supporting details focus.

LOW COMPLEXITY TEXTS (460L-600L)

1. Helpful Animals

Low: 460L

Service animals are helpful to people every day. Dogs can be trained to help people who cannot see. These special dogs wear harnesses that keep them connected to their owners. Some service animals help people who feel unsafe or fearful. Other animals can remind people to take medicine or help them walk. These amazing animals make life easier for many people.

Target Affixes: -ful (helpful), -ed (trained), -al (special), -ness (harness), -ed (connected), un- (unsafe), -ful (fearful), re- (remind), -ing (amazing), -er (easier)

Main Idea: Service animals help people in different ways

Supporting Details: Guiding the blind, helping with fear, reminding about medicine, making mobility easier

2. Weather Watchers

Low: 480L

Weather forecasters help us know what to expect each day. They use many helpful tools to predict if it will rain or shine. Some tools measure how much rain has fallen. Others record how fast the wind is blowing. Weather satellites take pictures from space to show clouds moving across Earth. With all this information, forecasters can tell us if we need an umbrella or sunscreen!

Target Affixes: -er (forecaster), -ful (helpful), pre- (predict), -ure (measure), -en (fallen), re- (record), -ing (blowing), -ite (satellite), -ing (moving), -tion (information), -er (forecaster), -ella (umbrella), sun- (sunscreen)

Main Idea: Weather forecasters use different tools to predict weather

Supporting Details: Tools that measure rain, record wind, satellites that take pictures from space

3. Underground Homes

Low: 490L

Many animals make their homes underground. Rabbits dig burrows with many tunnels and rooms. These underground homes keep rabbits safer from predators that want to eat them. Ants build amazing colonies with hundreds of connected tunnels. Prairie dogs create whole underground towns where they live together. Being underground helps these animals stay cooler in summer and warmer in winter.

Target Affixes: under- (underground), -ow (burrow), -el (tunnel), under- (underground), -er (safer), pre- (predator), -ing (amazing), -ies (colonies), -ed (connected), under- (underground), to- (together), under- (underground), -er (cooler), -er (warmer)

Main Idea: Many animals create homes underground for protection and comfort

Supporting Details: Rabbits dig burrows, ants build colonies, prairie dogs create towns, underground homes provide temperature control

4. The Water Cycle

Low: 510L

Water is always moving around our Earth in a cycle. The sun heats water in oceans, lakes, and rivers. The heated water turns into water vapor and rises into the sky. This is called evaporation. As water vapor cools, it forms clouds through condensation. When clouds get too heavy, water falls back to Earth as rain or snow. This is precipitation. The water then collects in oceans, lakes, and rivers, and the cycle restarts.

Target Affixes: -ing (moving), -s (heats), -ed (heated), -es (rises), -tion (evaporation), -s (cools), -tion (condensation), -y (heavy), -s (falls), pre- (precipitation), col- (collects), re- (restarts)

Main Idea: Water constantly moves through a cycle on Earth

Supporting Details: Evaporation of water into vapor, condensation into clouds, precipitation back to Earth's surface

5. Recycling Matters

Low: 520L

Recycling means using things again instead of throwing them away. Many items we use daily can be recycled. Paper can be reused to make new paper products. Plastic bottles can be remade into new bottles or clothing. Metal cans are melted down and reshaped. Recycling helps keep our planet cleaner and healthier. It also reduces the amount of trash in landfills. When we recycle, we are being helpful to Earth.

Target Affixes: re- (recycling), -ing (throwing), re- (recycled), re- (reused), re- (remade), -ing (clothing), -ed (melted), re- (reshaped), re- (recycling), -er (cleaner), -er (healthier), re- (reduces), land- (landfills), re- (recycle), -ful (helpful)

Main Idea: Recycling helps the environment by reusing materials

Supporting Details: Paper can be reused, plastic can be remade into new products, metal can be melted and reshaped, recycling reduces landfill waste

6. Plant Parts

Low: 530L

Plants have many important parts that help them grow and live. The roots grow underground and take in water and nutrients. The stem supports the plant and carries water up to the leaves. Leaves are like solar panels that collect sunlight to make food in a process called photosynthesis. Many plants grow colorful flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. After pollination, flowers turn into fruits or seeds that can grow into new plants.

Target Affixes: -s (roots), under- (underground), -ent (nutrients), -s (supports), -ies (carries), col- (collect), photo- (photosynthesis), -ful (colorful), at- (attract), -or (pollinator), butter- (butterfly), -tion (pollination)

Main Idea: Plants have different parts with specific functions

Supporting Details: Roots collect water and nutrients, stems support and transport, leaves collect sunlight, flowers attract pollinators

7. Animal Coverings

Low: 540L

Animals have different types of coverings that protect them. Birds have feathers that keep them warm and help them fly. Fish have scales that overlap like tiny shields. Mammals have fur or hair that can be thick or thin depending on where they live. Some animals like turtles have hardened shells for protection. Insects have exoskeletons, which are hard coverings on the outside of their bodies. These different coverings help animals survive in their homes.

Target Affixes: -ing (covering), pro- (protect), -er (feather), -m (warm), -s (scales), over- (overlap), -s (shields), -ck (thick), -in (thin), de- (depending), -en (hardened), -tion (protection), exo- (exoskeleton), -ing (covering), out- (outside), dif- (different), -ing (covering), sur- (survive)

Main Idea: Animals have different coverings that serve important purposes

Supporting Details: Birds have feathers, fish have scales, mammals have fur, turtles have shells, insects have exoskeletons

8. Super Senses

Low: 550L

Animals have amazing senses that help them survive. Dogs have an incredible sense of smell that is much stronger than ours. They can detect smells that are invisible to people. Eagles have powerful eyesight and can see tiny animals from high in the sky. Bats use echolocation, sending out sounds that bounce back to help them "see" in the dark. Some snakes can sense heat, allowing them to find warm animals even at night when it's completely dark.

Target Affixes: -ing (amazing), sur- (survive), in- (incredible), -er (stronger), de- (detect), in- (invisible), -ful (powerful), -sight (eyesight), echo- (echolocation), -ing (sending), -ce (bounce), -se (sense), -ing (allowing), com- (completely)

Main Idea: Animals have specialized senses that help them survive

Supporting Details: Dogs have strong smell, eagles have powerful vision, bats use echolocation, snakes sense heat

9. Money Basics

Low: 560L

Money helps us buy things we need and want. People earn money by working at jobs. When you receive money, you can spend it, save it, or donate it. Saving means keeping money for later use, like buying something special. Spending means using money to get something now. Donating means giving money to help others. It's important to make thoughtful choices about how to use money because it is limited.

Target Affixes: -rn (earn), -ing (working), re- (receive), -nd (spend), -ve (save), -ate (donate), -ing (saving), -ing (keeping), -er (later), -ing (buying), -al (special), -ing (spending), -ing (donating), -ing (giving), im- (important), -ful (thoughtful), -ed (limited)

Main Idea: Money can be earned and used in different ways

Supporting Details: People earn money through work, money can be spent now, saved for later, or donated to help others

10. How Seeds Travel

Low: 570L

Plants cannot move, but their seeds can travel in surprising ways. Some seeds have parachutes made of fluff that help them float on the wind. Dandelion seeds are carried this way. Some plants have seeds with hooks that attach to animal fur or people's clothing. These hitchhikers can travel far from their parent plant. Some plants package their seeds in tasty fruits. When animals eat the fruit, they deposit the seeds later in a new place. Water can also transport seeds to new locations.

Target Affixes: -el (travel), sur- (surprising), para- (parachute), -at (float), -ed (carried), -s (hooks), at- (attach), -ing (clothing), hitch- (hitchhiker), -el (travel), -age (package), -y (tasty), de- (deposit), trans- (transport), -tion (location)

Main Idea: Seeds travel away from parent plants in different ways

Supporting Details: Some seeds float on wind, attach to animals, get eaten and deposited elsewhere, or float on water

11. Animal Homes

Low: 580L

Animals build many different kinds of homes. Birds make nests by collecting twigs, grass, and other materials. Beavers are famous builders that cut down trees with their teeth to make dams and lodges. Some animals dig underground homes called burrows. Rabbits and groundhogs are good diggers. Bees work together to build honeycomb homes with many hexagonal rooms. Hermit crabs don't build homes at all—they find empty shells and move inside them. Each animal's home helps it survive in its environment.

Target Affixes: dif- (different), col- (collecting), -al (material), -ous (famous), -er (builder), under- (underground), ground- (groundhog), -er (digger), to- (together), honey- (honeycomb), hexa- (hexagonal), in- (inside), sur- (survive), -ment (environment)

Main Idea: Animals create or find different types of homes

Supporting Details: Birds build nests, beavers construct dams and lodges, some animals dig burrows, bees create honeycombs, hermit crabs use shells

12. All About Teeth

Low: 585L

Your teeth help you eat, speak, and smile. Each tooth has a hard outer covering called enamel that protects it. Inside each tooth are nerves that let you feel hot, cold, and pain. Different teeth have different jobs. Front teeth help bite food, while back teeth grind and crush it. Keeping your teeth healthy means brushing twice a day and flossing to remove food that gets stuck between teeth. Regular checkups with a dentist also help keep your smile bright.

Target Affixes: out- (outer), -ing (covering), pro- (protects), in- (inside), dif- (different), dif- (different), front- (front), -te (bite), back- (back), -nd (grind), -sh (crush), -ing (keeping), -y (healthy), -ing (brushing), -ing (flossing), re- (remove), -ck (stuck), be- (between), -ar (regular), check- (checkup), -ist (dentist), -ght (bright)

Main Idea: Teeth have different parts and functions that require proper care

Supporting Details: Teeth have protective enamel, different teeth have different jobs, teeth need regular brushing and flossing, dental checkups are important

13. Mighty Muscles

Low: 590L

Your body has over 600 muscles that help you move and do activities. Muscles work by contracting (getting shorter) and relaxing (getting longer). Some muscles you can control, like the ones that help you walk, jump, and smile. Other muscles work automatically, like your heart, which is a powerful muscle that pumps blood. Exercise makes muscles stronger and healthier. When you stretch before activity, you help prevent injuries. Resting is also important because it gives muscles time to repair and grow.

Target Affixes: -ity (activity), con- (contracting), -er (shorter), re- (relaxing), -er (longer), -lk (walk), -mp (jump), -le (smile), auto- (automatically), -ful (powerful), ex- (exercise), -er (stronger), -er (healthier), -ch (stretch), -ity (activity), pre- (prevent), -ies (injuries), -ing (resting), re- (repair)

Main Idea: Muscles have important functions and need proper care

Supporting Details: Muscles contract and relax to create movement, some muscles are voluntary while others work automatically, exercise strengthens muscles, rest helps muscles repair

14. Insect Bodies

Low: 595L

Insects are the most successful animals on Earth, with more kinds than any other animal group. All insects have three main body parts: the head, thorax, and abdomen. On their heads, insects have two antennae for sensing their surroundings. Most insects have compound eyes with many tiny lenses that help them see in different directions at once. Insects have six jointed legs attached to their thorax. Many insects have wings, which let them fly. Insects grow by molting, shedding their hard exoskeleton as they get bigger.

Target Affixes: -ful (successful), ab- (abdomen), -ae (antennae), -ing (sensing), sur- (surroundings), com- (compound), -es (lenses), dif- (different), -tion (direction), -ed (jointed), at- (attached), -ing (molting), -ing (shedding), exo- (exoskeleton), -er (bigger)

Main Idea: Insects
