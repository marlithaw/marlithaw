Philosophy & Approach

I lead with a systems-focused approach—creating learning ecosystems that cultivate growth, strengthen capacity, and transform lived experiences into sustainable leadership. Since beginning my journey as a Teach For America corps member in 2013, I've evolved my practice into a lasting commitment to fostering growth and impact that extend far beyond the classroom.

Over the past year, I stepped away from campus leadership to step into caregiving for my grandmother—a role that reinforced my commitment to sustainable systems, deepened my understanding of intergenerational healing, and strengthened my resolve to lead with integrity, even in uncertain terrain.

Systems Over Personalities

Throughout my career, I've championed the philosophy that effective educational leadership isn't about individual charisma or heroic efforts—it's about building robust systems that sustain excellence regardless of who's in the classroom.

My approach centers on creating instructional frameworks, professional development structures, and data systems that work together to support consistent growth—even during periods of transition, staffing challenges, or leadership changes.

Layered Professional Development

Teacher growth requires differentiated support that responds to individual needs while maintaining clear, consistent expectations.

Responsive Intervention Systems

Student growth depends on targeted, data-informed supports that adapt to changing needs while maintaining instructional coherence.

Integrated Literacy Framework

Reading and writing instruction should be deeply connected, with skills taught explicitly and applied across content areas.

The Triangle Leadership System

My leadership framework balances individual strengths, system coherence, and collective intelligence—creating a structure that honors differences while maintaining high expectations.

Personality-Informed Coaching

I adapt professional support based on how educators process information and make decisions, creating growth pathways that respect individual learning preferences.

Data-Informed Decision Making

Every instructional decision is grounded in systematic analysis of multiple data points, ensuring resources are directed to areas of greatest impact.

The true measure of instructional leadership isn't just what happens when you're present, but what continues to happen in your absence. Building systems that sustain excellence regardless of staffing challenges represents the highest form of educational leadership.
