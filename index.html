Empowering Educators, Inspiring Learners

Welcome! I’m Marlitha Williams, an instructional leader and curriculum developer dedicated to helping every student thrive. I bring a data-driven, compassionate approach to education, fostering environments where both teachers and students feel inspired.

About Me

My Why

Growing up in a multilingual community, I saw firsthand how tailored instruction can transform student confidence. My passion is to build bridges for learners by designing inclusive, joyful learning experiences. As an educator and leader, I strive to empower teachers with the tools and strategies they need, rooted in empathy and equity.

Professional Journey

  • 2010 – B.A. in Education from State University.
  • 2012 – Classroom Teacher at Riverdale Elementary School.
  • 2016 – M.Ed. in Curriculum and Instruction.
  • 2018 – Instructional Coach, Greene County Schools.
  • 2021 – Director of Academic Intervention, District 27.

Core Values

  • Equity
    Every student deserves tailored support to succeed.
  • Empathy
    Teaching with understanding and compassion.
  • Collaboration
    Partnering with teachers, families, and communities.
  • Innovation
    Embracing creative, research-driven strategies.

Expertise & Portfolio

Curriculum Development

I design rigorous, culturally responsive curricula that meet diverse learners’ needs. For example, a literacy unit I developed led to a 12.3% increase in 5th grade reading scores.

Instructional Leadership

As a coach and leader, I mentor educators to implement best practices. I have led professional workshops for over 200 teachers, focusing on academic language and engagement strategies.

Data-Driven Intervention

I specialize in using assessment data to drive targeted support. By analyzing classroom and benchmark data, I've helped reduce achievement gaps; one intervention plan for struggling readers achieved a +15% performance gain over one academic year.
