Scripted Substitute Lesson Plan Example

A detailed, scripted lesson plan template designed for substitute teachers to ensure instructional continuity when the regular teacher is absent.

Lesson Overview

Grade Level:

4th Grade

Subject:

English Language Arts

Time Required:

45 minutes

Standards Addressed:

ELA.4.11B, ELA.4.11C

Lesson Structure

1. Introduction (5 minutes)

Say: "Today we're going to explore how prepositional phrases help us describe where things are in relation to other things. Prepositional phrases begin with words like 'in,' 'on,' 'under,' and 'beside.'"

Do: Write the word "PREPOSITION" on the board and underline "POSITION" to highlight how these words indicate position or relationship.

2. Guided Practice (15 minutes)

Say: "Let's look at some examples of prepositional phrases in sentences."

Do: Display the example sentences on the projector and have students identify the prepositional phrases.

Examples: "The book is on the shelf." "The cat hid under the bed." "The bird flew over the house."

3. Independent Practice (15 minutes)

Say: "Now you'll work on your own to find and create prepositional phrases."

Do: Distribute the worksheet from the folder labeled "Preposition Practice" and have students complete exercises 1-3.

Note: Students who finish early can work on the extension activity on page 2.

4. Closing (10 minutes)

Say: "Let's share some of the prepositional phrases you created."

Do: Call on 3-4 students to share their examples. Write them on the board.

Say: "Tomorrow we'll continue exploring how these phrases can make our writing more interesting and precise."

Materials Needed

  • Preposition Practice worksheets (in blue folder)
  • Document camera or projector
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Example sentences displayed on slides

Notes for the Substitute

If students have difficulty identifying prepositional phrases, remind them that they answer the questions "where," "when," or "in what manner."

Students with IEPs may receive assistance from Mrs. Johnson, the resource teacher, who will come during independent practice time.

Please leave completed work in the tray labeled "4th Grade ELA" in the main office.

Preposition Practice Planning Framework

Detailed planning framework for integrating preposition practice across subject areas with differentiated approaches for various learning needs.

Planning Framework Structure

This framework provides a systematic approach to planning preposition instruction across the curriculum, with scaffolded approaches for different proficiency levels.

Cross-Curricular Applications

Mathematics Integration

Using prepositional phrases to precisely describe:

  • Spatial relationships in geometry
  • Process steps in multi-step problems
  • Location on coordinate planes

Example: "The point is located at the intersection of the x-axis and the y-axis."

Science Integration

Using prepositional phrases to enhance:

  • Procedural writing in lab reports
  • Descriptions of natural phenomena
  • Explanations of cycles and processes

Example: "Water vapor rises through the atmosphere and condenses into clouds."

Social Studies Integration

Using prepositional phrases to enhance:

  • Geographic descriptions
  • Historical event narration
  • Cultural comparisons

Example: "The pioneers traveled across the plains toward the western territories."

Physical Education Integration

Using prepositional phrases to describe:

  • Movement directions
  • Body positioning
  • Game rules and procedures

Example: "Move the ball around the defender and shoot into the goal."

Differentiation Strategies

Emerging Level

  • Visual supports with labeled prepositions
  • Sentence frames with prepositions highlighted
  • Physical demonstrations of spatial relationships

Developing Level

  • Guided practice with prepositional phrase creation
  • Sorting activities for function classification
  • Cloze passages with prepositional options

Advanced Level

  • Multiple prepositional phrases in complex sentences
  • Analysis of function and impact on meaning
  • Style variations with prepositional placement

Assessment Approaches

Formative Assessment: Daily exit tickets where students identify, create, or explain the function of prepositional phrases.

Project-Based Assessment: Cross-curricular projects where students create instructional materials using precise prepositional phrases.

Summative Assessment: Writing samples evaluated for effective and varied use of prepositional phrases across different functions.

AI-Ready Literacy Implementation Plan

Strategic framework for integrating AI literacy alongside traditional reading and writing instruction, preparing students for an AI-integrated world.

Implementation Overview

This implementation plan outlines a strategic approach to developing AI literacy skills among students by building on traditional literacy foundations.

Core Principle

The same cognitive processes that make students effective readers and writers also enable them to effectively interact with AI tools. By making these connections explicit, we prepare students for both traditional and emerging literacy contexts.

Literacy-AI Connections

Syntactic Knowledge

Traditional: Understanding relationship structures in language

AI Application: Crafting effective prompts with proper structure

Audience Awareness

Traditional: Considering information needs of readers

AI Application: Understanding system capabilities and limitations

Revision Process

Traditional: Iterative refinement of writing

AI Application: Prompt refinement based on outputs

Precision in Language

Traditional: Exact meaning conveyance

AI Application: Parameter specification and constraint definition

Implementation Phases

Phase 1: Foundation Building (Grades K-2)

Focus: Developing core literacy skills with awareness of digital tools

  • Introduce basic concept that computers respond to instructions
  • Practice giving clear, specific directions (procedural writing)
  • Develop vocabulary for spatial and sequential relationships
  • Build foundational reading and writing skills with digital connections

Phase 2: Skill Development (Grades 3-5)

Focus: Expanding literacy with explicit connections to digital interaction

  • Compare human vs. computer understanding of instructions
  • Analyze how word choice affects outcomes in digital contexts
  • Practice refining instructions based on outcomes
  • Develop critical evaluation of computer-generated content
  • Create multimodal texts that incorporate digital elements

Phase 3: Integration (Grades 6-8)

Focus: Sophisticated application of literacy skills in AI contexts

  • Introduce explicit prompt engineering techniques
  • Analyze how AI interprets and responds to different inputs
  • Develop ethical understanding of AI capabilities and limitations
  • Practice collaborative writing with AI assistance
  • Create projects that integrate AI tools as appropriate

Sample Lesson Framework

Grade 4: "Precision in Instructions"

Objective: Students will understand how specificity in language affects outcomes in both human and digital contexts.

Activity 1: "Human Computer" - One student gives instructions while another acts as a "computer" following only exactly what is said.

Activity 2: Students compare vague vs. specific instructions and predict outcomes.

Activity 3: Students revise a set of instructions to make them more precise and effective.

Connection: Explicit discussion of how this skill applies to both writing for human audiences and creating instructions for digital tools.

Assessment Strategies

  • Performance tasks requiring clear instructional writing
  • Comparative analysis of vague vs. specific language outcomes
  • Projects demonstrating appropriate digital tool integration
  • Reflective writing on the connection between traditional and digital literacy
  • Portfolio development showing progression of skills

Literacy Case Study: Cohort C

Detailed case study analyzing the implementation and outcomes of literacy interventions for a specific student cohort that faced significant challenges.

Case Study Overview

This case study documents the literacy journey of "Cohort C" - a group of 47 fifth-grade students who began the academic year significantly behind grade-level expectations, with 72% reading at least two years below grade level. The study details the systematic interventions implemented, challenges encountered, and remarkable growth achieved over an 18-month period.

Initial Assessment Data

Reading Proficiency (Beginning of Year)

  • 8% Meeting grade level standards
  • 20% One year below grade level
  • 45% Two years below grade level
  • 27% Three+ years below grade level

Specific Skill Gaps

  • 83% Deficient in multisyllabic decoding
  • 76% Below benchmark in reading fluency
  • 91% Limited academic vocabulary
  • 87% Struggling with inferential comprehension

Contextual Challenges

  • High mobility rate (31% of students new to the school)
  • Inconsistent literacy instruction in previous grades
  • 42% of students identified for special education services
  • Limited access to literacy materials at home (68% reporting)
  • High rates of chronic absenteeism (23% of cohort)

Intervention Approach

Three-Tier Intervention System

Tier 1: Enhanced Core Instruction (All Students)
  • Structured literacy approach with systematic skill progression
  • Increased time allocation (120 minutes daily literacy block)
  • Text complexity gradient with scaffolded supports
  • Accountable talk protocols for academic discourse
  • Integrated content-area literacy strategies
Tier 2: Targeted Skill Interventions (42% of Students)
  • Daily 30-minute small group interventions (3-5 students)
  • Skill-specific focus based on diagnostic assessment
  • Explicit instruction in identified deficit areas
  • Bi-weekly progress monitoring with adjustment
  • Technology-supported practice with immediate feedback
Tier 3: Intensive Intervention (27% of Students)
  • One-on-one daily intervention (20 minutes)
  • Multisensory structured literacy approach
  • Personalized learning plans with specific, measurable goals
  • Weekly progress monitoring with immediate instructional adjustment
  • Parent/caregiver training component

Key Implementation Components

Assessment System

  • Comprehensive diagnostic assessment
  • Weekly skill-based progress monitoring
  • Monthly benchmark assessments
  • Running records with miscue analysis
  • Digital tracking dashboard

Teacher Support

  • Intensive professional development (42 hours)
  • Weekly collaborative planning
  • Bi-weekly coaching cycles
  • Video analysis of instruction
  • Data analysis protocols

Student Engagement

  • Goal-setting conferences
  • Progress visualization tools
  • Interest-based text selection
  • Self-monitoring strategies
  • Celebration of growth milestones

Outcomes Analysis

End-of-Year Results

Reading Proficiency Gains
  • 53% Meeting grade level standards (+45 percentage points)
  • 29% One year below grade level
  • 14% Two years below grade level
  • 4% Three+ years below grade level

92% of students showed at least 1.5 years growth

Skill-Specific Improvements
  • Multisyllabic decoding: 91% at/above benchmark
  • Reading fluency: 83% at/above benchmark
  • Academic vocabulary: 76% at/above benchmark
  • Inferential comprehension: 72% at/above benchmark

Average gain of 22 words per minute in oral reading fluency

State Assessment Performance

On end-of-year state assessments, Cohort C achieved:

  • 77% Approaches Grade Level (vs. 79% state average)
  • 59% Meets Grade Level (vs. 55% state average)
  • 30% Masters Grade Level (vs. 29% state average)

This represents the first time in five years that the school matched or exceeded state averages, despite starting significantly behind.

Key Success Factors

  1. Systematic and explicit instruction focused on identified skill deficits
  2. Tiered intervention model with appropriate intensity based on need
  3. Responsive data systems that triggered immediate instructional adjustments
  4. Consistent implementation with high fidelity to instructional protocols
  5. Collaborative teacher support that built capacity and sustained momentum
  6. Student engagement strategies that fostered ownership of learning

Sustainability Measures

To ensure continued progress, the following systems were established:

  • Documented intervention protocols incorporated into school literacy plan
  • Train-the-trainer model to maintain instructional expertise
  • Data tracking systems embedded in school-wide practices
  • Vertical team structure to support transition to middle school
  • Resource allocation priorities adjusted to maintain intervention intensity
