My Journey

I lead with a systems-focused approach—creating learning ecosystems that cultivate growth, strengthen capacity, and transform lived experiences into sustainable leadership. Since beginning my journey as a Teach For America corps member in 2013, I've evolved my practice into a lasting commitment to fostering growth and impact that extend far beyond the classroom.

Over the past year, I stepped away from campus leadership to step into caregiving for my grandmother—a role that reinforced my commitment to sustainable systems, deepened my understanding of intergenerational healing, and strengthened my resolve to lead with integrity, even in uncertain terrain.

Philosophy & Approach

Systems Over Personalities

I believe that effective educational leadership is about creating robust systems that transcend individual personalities. When instructional frameworks are built with intention and clarity, they produce consistent results regardless of staffing challenges.

Capacity Building

My approach centers on building capacity in others through personalized coaching and targeted support. By honoring individuals' unique strengths while providing clear structures, I develop teams that exceed the sum of their parts.

Beyond the Classroom

My background spans education and law, bringing a unique perspective to educational leadership. This interdisciplinary experience informs my approach to systems design, equity, and sustainable impact.

Prepositional Phrases Instructional Framework

This comprehensive framework provides a structured approach to teaching prepositional phrases across grade levels through cross-curricular applications.

Framework Overview

  • Vertically aligned progression from basic to advanced usage
  • Cross-curricular integration strategies (math, science, social studies)
  • Assessment protocols and rubrics
  • Differentiation strategies for various learning needs

Key Components

Introduction to Prepositional Phrases

Foundational concepts that help students identify and understand prepositions and their objects, distinguishing them from other grammatical structures.

Functions in Sentences

Explores how prepositional phrases function as adjectival and adverbial modifiers, enhancing sentence clarity and complexity.

Integration with Writing Process

Strategies for incorporating prepositional phrases into the drafting and revision stages of writing for more descriptive and precise text.

Cross-Curricular Applications

Examples of how prepositional phrases enhance mathematical explanations, scientific procedures, and historical narratives.

Scripted Substitute Lesson Plan Example

A detailed, ready-to-use lesson plan template designed for substitute teachers to ensure instructional continuity when the regular classroom teacher is absent.

Plan Features

  • Minute-by-minute timing guidelines
  • Scripted instructions and questions
  • Built-in differentiation options
  • Formative assessment checkpoints
  • Extension activities for early finishers

Lesson Structure

1. Introduction & Hook (5 minutes)

Engaging scenario that introduces the concept of prepositional phrases through a real-world context that students can visualize.

2. Direct Instruction (10 minutes)

Clear explanation of what prepositional phrases are, with examples and non-examples to build conceptual understanding.

3. Guided Practice (15 minutes)

Structured activities where students work with teacher guidance to identify and create basic prepositional phrases.

4. Independent Practice (15 minutes)

Application activities where students demonstrate their understanding through writing and revision tasks.

5. Assessment & Closure (5 minutes)

Quick formative assessment to gauge understanding, followed by summary of key learning points.

Special Notes for Substitutes

This lesson is designed to be accessible to teachers without specific grammar expertise. All necessary background information is provided, along with potential student misconceptions and how to address them.

AI-Ready Literacy Implementation Plan

This forward-thinking framework connects traditional literacy instruction with the skills needed for effective AI interaction, creating a comprehensive approach to prepare students for an AI-integrated future.

Implementation Overview

The AI-Ready Literacy model recognizes that many of the same skills that make students effective readers and writers will enable them to effectively leverage AI tools in their academic and professional lives.

Key Literacy-AI Connections

Syntactic Knowledge

Traditional: Understanding relationship structures in text

AI Application: Effective prompt engineering and query formulation

Audience Awareness

Traditional: Considering reader needs when writing

AI Application: Understanding AI system capabilities and limitations

Revision Process

Traditional: Iterative refinement of written work

AI Application: Output assessment and prompt refinement

Precision in Language

Traditional: Conveying exact meaning through word choice

AI Application: Parameter specification and constraint definition

Implementation Timeline

Phase 1: Foundation Building

Weeks 1-4

Introduce core literacy concepts with explicit connections to future AI applications. Build awareness of how clear communication applies to both human and machine interactions.

Phase 2: Skill Development

Weeks 5-12

Develop specific literacy skills with parallel AI-interaction skills. Students practice both traditional writing and basic prompt engineering through structured exercises.

Phase 3: Integration & Application

Weeks 13-18

Apply integrated literacy skills to authentic projects. Students demonstrate mastery through creating content that shows both traditional literacy proficiency and AI-interaction capability.
