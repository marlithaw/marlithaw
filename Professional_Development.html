Sustainable Instructional Leadership

Coaching Excellence Through Personalized Leadership

A case study of leadership development with predominantly inexperienced teachers

Marlitha Williams | Educational Leader & Instructional Coach

The Challenge

Teaching Team Profile (2022-2024)

During this period, IDEA Brackenridge Academy faced a significant staffing challenge with 83% of staff having fewer than 3 years of teaching experience and 72% being uncertified teachers with no formal pedagogical training.

High-Stakes Expectations

Despite these staffing limitations, the campus was still expected to maintain high academic performance on state assessments, with accountability consequences for failing to meet growth targets.

Minimal Instructional Background

Most teachers had minimal knowledge of literacy development and teaching methodologies, requiring comprehensive support systems and intensive coaching to build capacity quickly.

Strategic Approach

The Triangle Leadership System

Inspired by Phil Jackson's leadership philosophy, I developed a "Triangle Leadership System" where:

Individual Strengths

Identified and leveraged through personalized coaching informed by Myers-Briggs assessments

System Coherence

Clear structures and routines that accommodated different teaching styles while maintaining instructional integrity

Collective Intelligence

Collaborative practices that allowed the team to exceed the sum of individual capabilities

Myers-Briggs Integration: Personality-Informed Coaching

Understanding that each teacher processed information differently, I adapted my coaching approach based on personality preferences:

Introversion (I)

31% of staff

One-on-one coaching sessions with advance preparation materials

Extraversion (E)

69% of staff

Collaborative planning with opportunities to verbalize thinking

Sensing (S)

58% of staff

Concrete examples, model lessons, and step-by-step guidance

Intuition (N)

42% of staff

Big-picture frameworks with autonomy to customize implementation

Implementation

Tiered Coaching Framework

Tier 1: Foundation Building (All Teachers)

  • Weekly grade-level team planning with scripted lesson guidance
  • Biweekly classroom walkthroughs with immediate verbal feedback
  • Monthly professional development focused on core teaching competencies
  • Standardized lesson plan templates with embedded scaffolds

Tier 2: Targeted Development (Struggling/New Teachers)

  • Twice-weekly co-teaching or model lessons
  • Daily quick checks with "bite-sized" action steps
  • Video analysis with guided self-reflection
  • Skill-focused learning walks with peer teachers

Tier 3: Advanced Leadership (High-Performing Teachers)

  • Mentorship responsibilities with recognition systems
  • Action research opportunities targeting campus needs
  • Leadership roles in professional development delivery
  • Collaborative curriculum adaptation projects

Phil Jackson-Inspired Leadership Principles

Mindful Leadership

Beginning meetings with brief centering practices to focus attention and reduce stress

Distributed Authority

Empowering teacher-leaders rather than centralizing all decisions

Process Over Outcomes

Emphasizing quality instruction rather than just test scores

Strengths-Based Development

Building on what teachers do well rather than fixating on deficits

Evidence-Based Foundations

This coaching framework was grounded in educational research on effective professional development:

Job-Embedded Learning

Coaching integrated within daily teaching practices (Darling-Hammond, 2017)

Active Learning

Teachers practicing techniques rather than passive reception (Garet et al., 2001)

Coherence

Alignment between coaching, curriculum, and evaluation (Desimone, 2009)

Adult Learning Theory

Respecting prior experience and promoting self-direction (Knowles, 1984)

Strategic Application

The ultimate validation of this coaching framework was not simply improved metrics, but watching passionate but inexperienced individuals transform into confident, capable educators who could articulate both what they were teaching and why they were teaching it. By creating systems that honored individual strengths while providing clear structure, we built a teaching team that could thrive despite limited formal training.
