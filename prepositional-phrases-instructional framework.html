Introduction

This comprehensive instructional package demonstrates a vertically aligned approach to teaching prepositional phrases across grades 3-5. It integrates research-based principles from both the Science of Reading and the Science of Writing while implementing the Gradual Release of Responsibility model to scaffold instruction appropriately for diverse learners.

Research Foundation

This package draws from Dr. Young-Suk Kim's research on the interdependence of reading and writing development, specifically:

  • Reading and writing share foundational linguistic knowledge
  • Writing reinforces reading comprehension by requiring students to actively construct meaning
  • Explicit instruction in language structures enhances both reading and writing proficiency

Throughout this package, you'll find materials that progress in complexity from grade 3 to grade 5, with consistent visual supports, instructional routines, and assessment tools that maintain coherence while acknowledging developmental differences.

Integration of Science of Reading and Science of Writing

Science of Reading Elements

  • Vocabulary Development: Explicit teaching of prepositions as Tier 2 words
  • Syntactic Awareness: Understanding sentence structure and organization
  • Reading Comprehension: Using prepositional phrases to visualize and understand text
  • Fluency: Recognizing phrase boundaries for appropriate phrasing
  • Visualization: Using spatial/temporal language to create mental images

Science of Writing Elements

  • Transcription: Correctly forming prepositional phrases in writing
  • Ideation: Using prepositional phrases to add precision to ideas
  • Elaboration: Extending simple sentences with prepositional details
  • Revision: Improving writing by adding or refining prepositional phrases
  • Audience Awareness: Understanding how details affect reader comprehension

Research-Based Integration Points

Based on Dr. Young-Suk Kim's Direct and Indirect Effects Model of Writing (DIEW), this package integrates reading and writing through:

  1. Shared Knowledge Base: Students analyze prepositional phrases in reading before producing them in writing
  2. Reciprocal Skill Development: Reading examples inform writing, and writing reinforces comprehension
  3. Cognitive Load Management: Scaffolded progression from recognition to production
  4. Executive Function Support: Structured templates and revision strategies

Gradual Release Model Implementation

The instructional sequence for each grade level follows the Gradual Release of Responsibility model, systematically transferring ownership from teacher to students:

I Do

Teacher models and demonstrates

  • Explicit modeling of identification
  • Think-aloud demonstrations
  • Clear visual annotation examples
  • Purpose explanation and rationale

We Do

Teacher and students work together

  • Guided practice with feedback
  • Collaborative annotation
  • Shared writing experiences
  • Group discussion and analysis

You Do Together

Students practice with peers

  • Partner practice activities
  • Peer feedback opportunities
  • Collaborative creation tasks
  • Structured group work

You Do Alone

Independent application

  • Individual practice tasks
  • Independent creation
  • Self-assessment
  • Application to new contexts

Differentiation Decision Points

Effective implementation requires responsive instruction that addresses diverse student needs. The following decision points guide differentiation throughout the instructional sequence:

Pre-Assessment Decision Points

If students... Then...
Cannot identify prepositions Begin with preposition word sort activities and basic identification
Can identify but not explain function Use visual supports for function categories (where/when/how icons)
Can explain function but not create Provide sentence frames and guided practice opportunities
Can create but not analyze purpose Use think-alouds to model purpose analysis
Show advanced understanding Move to extension activities with multiple phrases and author's craft

During-Instruction Decision Points

If you observe... Then...
Confusion about what constitutes a phrase Provide visual boundaries using color-coding or brackets
Difficulty connecting to meaning Use visual supports showing the relationship explained by the preposition
Limited vocabulary for prepositions Create a visual preposition word bank with examples
Challenges with creating original phrases Offer phrase frames with blanks for objects only
Ready for more complex applications Introduce multiple prepositional phrases in a single sentence

Connections to Author's Purpose and Text Analysis

A distinctive feature of this instructional framework is its explicit connection between prepositional phrases and author's purpose. As students progress through the grades, they develop increasingly sophisticated understanding of how prepositional phrases serve the writer's intentions.

Author's Purpose Framework

Students learn to analyze prepositional phrases through the lens of the author's purpose:

To Inform

Prepositional phrases add precision to factual information

Example: "The water cycle occurs throughout the atmosphere."

To Entertain

Prepositional phrases create vivid imagery

Example: "The cat darted under the sofa with lightning speed."

To Persuade

Prepositional phrases emphasize key points

Example: "Students succeed with proper support from dedicated teachers."

Text Analysis Progression

Grade 3: Noticing how prepositional phrases add detail to captions and simple texts

Grade 4: Analyzing how different types of prepositional phrases serve different functions in a text

Grade 5: Evaluating how prepositional phrases contribute to author's style and purpose across genres

Annotation Framework

Students use a consistent color-coding system across grades:

  • Where phrases (blue)
  • When phrases (orange)
  • How phrases (green)

This consistent system reinforces the function-based approach to prepositional phrases while supporting visual learners.

Analysis Questions Across Grades

Grade Basic Analysis Deep Analysis Connection to Author's Purpose
Grade 3 "What information does this prepositional phrase add?" "How does the picture help us understand the prepositional phrase?" "Why did the author add this detail about where?"
Grade 4 "What function does this prepositional phrase serve?" "How would the meaning change if we removed or changed this phrase?" "How does this prepositional phrase help the author's message?"
Grade 5 "How do multiple prepositional phrases work together in this text?" "How does the author use prepositional phrases differently in narrative versus informational text?" "What effect was the author trying to create with these prepositional phrases?"

Sample Text Analysis Activity (Grade 5)

Students examine paired texts on the same topic:

Informational Text

"Rainforests grow near the equator. The vegetation thrives throughout the year due to abundant rainfall. Animals live in different layers of the forest, from the forest floor to the emergent layer."

Narrative Text

"Maya crept through the dense undergrowth. During the early morning mist, the rainforest came alive with mysterious sounds. A brightly colored bird swooped over her head and disappeared into the emerald canopy."

Analysis Questions:

  1. How do the prepositional phrases differ between the informational and narrative texts?
  2. What does this tell us about the authors' different purposes?
  3. How could you revise either text by changing the prepositional phrases to strengthen the author's purpose?

Teacher Notes: Grade 3 Prepositional Phrases

Grade 5 Instructional Materials

Crafting with Prepositional Phrases: Author's Tools for Impact

Learning Goal: I can analyze and create prepositional phrases to achieve specific author's purposes across text types.

Part A: Analyzing Author's Purpose

Read each paragraph. Underline all prepositional phrases and analyze how they support the author's purpose.

Informational Text: "Coral Reefs"

Coral reefs exist in warm, shallow waters near coastlines. During daylight hours, coral polyps work with algae to produce nutrients. Thousands of marine species make their homes within the reef structure. Scientists study these ecosystems with specialized equipment throughout the year.

Author's Purpose: To inform readers about coral reefs

How do the prepositional phrases help achieve this purpose?

(Student would analyze how phrases add precision and factual detail to support informational purpose)

Author's Purpose: To entertain readers with an adventure story

How do the prepositional phrases help achieve this purpose?

Part B: Revising for Author's Purpose

Revise the following paragraph by adding prepositional phrases that strengthen the author's purpose.

Original Paragraph (Purpose: To persuade)

"Students should have recess. Physical activity is important. Breaks help students focus. Learning happens when children play. Our school needs to protect recess time."

Revised Paragraph (add at least 5 prepositional phrases)

(Student would revise to add persuasive prepositional phrases)

Explain your revision choices:
  1. What types of prepositional phrases did you add (where/when/how)?
  2. How do these phrases strengthen the persuasive purpose?
  3. Which phrase do you think is most effective and why?
